Air Travel Systememic Failures

In-flight Travel Experience & Connection

The experience wasn't all bad. In terms of end result it was actually ideal. The problems I highlighted regarding seat assignment and checkin lockout adversely impacted victims of process and/or software bugs are significant. I think they should be address very quickly by American Airlines, but the in-flight travel experience and connection experience was very good. Generally.

Punctuality

For me the delay in arriving into ORD wasn't impactful. My connection was only tight for me because I needed to get a seat assignment at the gate, but I didn't need to run there. However, we left from CMI significantly later (even though we were on the plane on time).

Connection Priority

The first leg of my trip was interesting when we were deplaning because it was the first time I had heard the flight attendant request that other passengers on the flight let the Cleveland passenger out first as their connection was very agressive with the delay.

This was great to see happen. I have previously been in this situation before but that never happened for me. I ended up missing my connection to SFO that day.

One day you may need this priority. So don't whine about someone at the back needing deplaning priority. Instead embrace it. We are all in this together.

What was even better about this (I thought) was that the airline/flight attendant knew this was the case. They are starting to monitor connections of passengers to streamline critical connections first. I think? Maybe I am wrong though. If it is the case though, then this is putting the passenger first.

Suggestions for Improvements

I do however have a suggestion to make this new practice better for all involved.

First off, the flight attendant should know where these passengers are located and inform all rows 10 and lower to let the passenger from 10C out first. Not just "please let the Cleveland connection passenger out first". I have no knowledge of where the Cleveland passenger is located. Make it easier for other passengers to follow the directives. I had been putting my laptop away in my bag when the passenger from Cleveland walked past me so I didn't know I was no longer waiting on him/her to pass until I asked a neighboring passenger when everyone else was standing up.

If the critical connection passenger has valet baggage, prioritize it for them, or this is all moot.
